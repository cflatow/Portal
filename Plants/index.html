Garden Care Guide

Care instructions for the plants in my garden. Quick reference for watering, sunlight, and seasonal notes.

Vegetables

Tomatoes

Full sun lovers that produce best with consistent moisture and warmth. Stake or cage early.

Sun: 6–8 hrs full sun Water: 1–2 in/week, deep Season: Plant after last frost

Basil

Pinch flowers to keep leaves coming. Pairs well with tomatoes in the bed and on the plate.

Sun: 6+ hrs full sun Water: When top inch is dry Season: Warm weather only

Lettuce

Cool-season crop. Bolts in heat — succession-plant every 2 weeks in spring and fall.

Sun: Partial shade in summer Water: Keep soil evenly moist Season: Spring and fall

Peppers

Slow starters that reward patience. Need warm soil to take off.

Sun: 6–8 hrs full sun Water: 1–2 in/week Season: Transplant when nights stay above 55°F

Herbs

Rosemary

Drought-tolerant Mediterranean perennial. Hates wet feet — plant in well-draining soil.

Sun: Full sun Water: Sparingly, let dry between Season: Perennial in zones 7+

Mint

Vigorous spreader — keep it in a container unless you want a mint lawn.

Sun: Partial shade to full sun Water: Regular, keep moist Season: Perennial

Parsley

Biennial grown as an annual. Cut outer stems first and the plant keeps producing.

Sun: Full sun to partial shade Water: 1 in/week Season: Spring through fall

Flowers

Marigolds

Easy annual that deters some garden pests. Deadhead spent blooms for continuous flowering.

Sun: Full sun Water: Moderate, drought-tolerant Season: Spring through frost

Sunflowers

Direct-sow after last frost. Tall varieties may need staking in windy spots.

Sun: Full sun Water: Deep, weekly Season: Summer to early fall

Lavender

Perennial that wants sun, gravelly soil, and minimal fuss. Prune after bloom to keep tidy.

Sun: Full sun Water: Low, drought-tolerant Season: Perennial in zones 5–9
